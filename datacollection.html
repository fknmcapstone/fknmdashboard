Track 2 - Data Collection

COVID-19 had a major impact on school feeding across Canada, and its effects were documented across many geographical areas, via many data collection methods. These methods were analysed to produce the main results. This page describes how these data sources were analysed and the dimensions of focus for the different sources.

  • 166 citations initially retrieved
  • 34 articles and 1 seminar fulfilled eligibility criteria
  • Eligible documents sourced from 7 provinces/territories
  • 10 of the articles sourced were done so from social media

  • 35 sources referenced, and consideration of the ANGELO Dimensions tallied as shown
  • Examples of Dimension components:
  • Physical - food availabiity, accessibility, distribution
  • Economic - food costs, price gouging, available financial resources
  • Political - government intervention (creation of new laws and regulations)
  • Sociocultural - attitudes, values, collaborations of community and society

  • 35 sources referenced, and consideration of the GTE Dimensions tallied as shown
  • Examples of Dimension components:
  • Increase healthy options - distribution of free meal kits according to SFP guidelines
  • Reduce deterrents - providing food at accessible locations, following COVID-19 guidelines, non-discriminatory outreach
  • Improve social and economic resources - creation of new SFPs duing COVID-19, increased funding
  • Build on community capacity - collaborations between community members, using community resources in beneficial ways
